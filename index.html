Work Experience

Indiana University - Media Digitization Preservation Initiative

Audio Preservation Engineer

July 2016-Present

Preservation transfer and digitization of analogue audio lacquer discs and open reel tapes. Restoration of DAT transfer files with Pro Tools. Digitized difficult open reel tapes suffering from various age-related issues such as soft binder syndrome, poor physical condition, and complicated track configurations.

Indiana University Archives of Traditional Music

Head of Recording Services

2010-2018

Managed, trained, supervised, and quality checked four graduate assistants in the handling of archival sound recordings, open reel tapes, cassettes, etc. to fill orders; recognizing format characteristics, operation and maintenance of playback machines. Digitized the extensive Hoagy Carmichael Collection that included 750 recordings featuring Carmichael as composer or performer in the form of lacquer, shellac, and cardboard discs, open reel tapes, and cassettes.

Indiana University Traditional Arts Indiana

Technical Assistant

2000-2009

Helped develop their Microsoft Access database of traditional artists. Maintained their website. Conducted traditional artist survey in Indiana by documenting their crafts, interviewing, taking photos, recording audio, and creating artist profiles on the TAI website.
